Here are the research references underpinning each component:

Key Compatibility — Krumhansl-Kessler Profiles

Krumhansl, C. L. (1990). Cognitive Foundations of Musical Pitch. Oxford University Press.
The 12-element probe-tone rating profiles (major and minor) are from Chapter 2. Inter-key similarity = Pearson correlation between rotated profiles.
Tempo Compatibility — Weber's Law

Drake, C. & Botte, M.-C. (1993). "Tempo sensitivity in auditory sequences: Evidence for a multiple-look model." Perception & Psychophysics, 54(3), 277–286.
JND for tempo ≈ 4% of reference BPM. We use Gaussian decay with σ = 3 × JND.
Timbre (MFCC) — Bhattacharyya Distance + Coefficient

Aucouturier, J.-J. & Pachet, F. (2002). "Music Similarity Measures: What's the Use?" Proceedings of ISMIR 2002.
Single-Gaussian MFCC model. Bhattacharyya coefficient BC = exp(-D_B) as the principled distance-to-similarity conversion.
MFCC c₀ exclusion

Berenzweig, A. et al. (2004). "A Large-Scale Evaluation of Acoustic and Subjective Music-Similarity Measures." Computer Music Journal, 28(2), 63–76.
Drop c₀ from MFCC distance to avoid double-counting with loudness.
General MIR context / weight structure

Knees, P. & Schedl, M. (2013). Music Similarity and Retrieval. Springer.
MFCCs as dominant signal for timbral similarity, multi-component weighted scoring.
